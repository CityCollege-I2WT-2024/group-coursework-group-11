What You Can Do

Practicing Good Privacy Hygiene

Keeping your online privacy in check is essential. Start by using strong, unique passwords for each of your accounts and consider employing a password manager to keep track of them. Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) wherever possible to add an extra layer of security. Regularly update your software and apps to protect against vulnerabilities. Be cautious about the information you share online and adjust your privacy settings to control who can see your posts and personal details.

Limiting Your Digital Footprint

Reducing your digital footprint helps protect your personal information. Begin by auditing your online presence: search for yourself to see what information is publicly available. Delete old accounts and unsubscribe from mailing lists you no longer use. Adjust privacy settings on your social media profiles to limit the amount of personal information visible to others. Be mindful of the data you share online, and consider using a virtual private network (VPN) to enhance your privacy when browsing.

Identifying and Avoiding Scams

Staying vigilant against online scams is crucial. Be skeptical of unsolicited messages or emails, especially those requesting personal information or urgent action. Avoid clicking on links or downloading attachments from unknown sources. Familiarize yourself with common scam tactics, such as phishing and fake delivery notifications, to better recognize and avoid them. If something seems suspicious, verify the information through official channels before taking any action.
