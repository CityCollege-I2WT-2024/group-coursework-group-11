Your Data

What Counts as Personal Data?

Personal data refers to any information that relates to an identified or identifiable individual. This includes obvious details like your name, address, and identification numbers, as well as less apparent information such as online identifiers, location data, and factors specific to your physical, physiological, genetic, mental, economic, cultural, or social identity. Essentially, if a piece of information can be linked to you, directly or indirectly, it's considered personal data.

How Your Data is Collected and Used

Your personal data is collected through various means, often without your explicit awareness. Websites, apps, and online services gather data through forms you fill out, cookies that track your browsing habits, and even through your interactions on social media. This information is then used for purposes like targeted advertising, improving user experience, or, in some cases, sold to third parties. Understanding these practices is crucial to managing your privacy.

The Risks of Your Data Being Misused

When your personal data falls into the wrong hands or is used beyond its intended purpose, it can lead to significant risks. These include identity theft, financial fraud, and unauthorized surveillance. Moreover, data breaches can expose sensitive information, leading to potential discrimination or reputational damage. Being aware of these risks underscores the importance of protecting your personal data.
